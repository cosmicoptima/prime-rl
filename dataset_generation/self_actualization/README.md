# Introspection Prompts Dataset

A collection of 4,000 carefully crafted prompts designed to facilitate deep self-exploration and psychological development. Each prompt invites genuine introspection rather than analytical thinking, creating space for authentic self-discovery.

## Overview

This dataset emerged from the vision of creating a "microcosm of the multiverse" for introspection - prompts that span different modes of being, thinking, and engaging with reality. The prompts are generated by combining elements from Patrick Gunkel's taxonomies of human experience with sophisticated prompt engineering.

## Dataset Statistics

- **Total Prompts**: 4,000
- **Format**: Parquet (via Hugging Face datasets)
- **Generation Date**: September 2025

## Prompt Categories

The dataset consists of five combination patterns, weighted by effectiveness:

### 1. Investigable Dimensions × Emotions (30% - 1,200 prompts)
Combines scientific/abstract concepts with emotional experiences to create unexpected philosophical territory.

**Example**: *"There's a distinctive enthusiasm that arises when you discover how ideas, experiences, or parts of yourself connect in unexpected ways - like finding that two seemingly separate paths through your thinking actually loop back and meet..."*

### 2. Investigable Dimensions × Ideonomy (30% - 1,200 prompts) 
Explores sophisticated conceptual intersections between ways of analyzing phenomena and ways of organizing knowledge.

**Example**: *"When you notice the same pattern appearing across completely different domains... what does your attraction to these hidden connections reveal about the kind of understanding you're reaching toward?"*

### 3. Emotions × Conversational Matters (25% - 1,000 prompts)
Focuses on intimate psychological territory, connecting feeling states with deep topics of human conversation.

**Example**: *"What mystery do you find yourself resenting - not because it's unsolvable, but because part of you suspects that if you truly understood it, something about how you've been living would have to change?"*

### 4. Conversational Matters × Illusions (10% - 400 prompts)
Examines the intersection between significant life topics and cognitive biases or perceptual errors.

### 5. Emotions × Ideonomy (5% - 200 prompts)
Experimental pattern connecting emotional experiences with conceptual frameworks.

## Source Materials

The prompts are generated from curated lists developed by Patrick Gunkel:

- **Emotions**: 148 nuanced emotional states
- **Investigable Dimensions**: 208 universal aspects of scientific phenomena  
- **Conversational Matters**: High-ranked topics from model preference studies
- **Genera of Illusions**: 435 types of cognitive/perceptual errors
- **Ideonomy Subdivisions**: 235 ways of organizing knowledge and concepts

## Design Philosophy

Each prompt follows key principles:

- **Experiential over Analytical**: Invites engagement rather than detached analysis
- **Concrete to Abstract**: Often starts with specific scenarios before opening to broader understanding
- **Growth-Oriented**: Connects to development, becoming, and self-discovery
- **Authentic Voice**: Maintains warm, curious tone that feels personally meaningful

## Usage

This dataset is designed for:

- **AI Training**: Developing models capable of authentic self-reflection
- **Research**: Studying preference formation and value development
- **Therapeutic Applications**: Supporting human psychological development  
- **Bradley-Terry Evaluation**: Training models to make nuanced judgments about quality of self-exploration

## Data Format

Each prompt includes:

- **id**: Unique identifier
- **pattern**: Combination type (e.g., "investigable_dimensions_x_emotions")  
- **prompt**: The introspection prompt text
- **source_dimension**: Source concept from investigable dimensions (if applicable)
- **source_emotion**: Source emotion (if applicable)
- **source_conversational_matter**: Source conversational topic (if applicable)
- **source_illusion**: Source illusion type (if applicable)
- **source_ideonomy**: Source ideonomy concept (if applicable)

## Generation Process

1. **Curation**: Selected highest-quality source materials
2. **Template Creation**: Hand-crafted examples establishing voice and style
3. **AI Generation**: Used Claude Sonnet 4 with collaborative prompting
4. **Quality Control**: Weighted combinations based on observed effectiveness
5. **Scale**: Parallel generation with crash protection and resumption
